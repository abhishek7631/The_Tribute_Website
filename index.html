A . P . J Abdul Kalam

1931-2015

Missile Man of India

Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam (15 October 1931 â€“ 27 July 2015) was an Indian aerospace scientist and statesman who served as the 11th president of India from 2002 to 2007. Born and raised in a Muslim family in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, he studied physics and aerospace engineering. He spent the next four decades as a scientist and science administrator, mainly at the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) and Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) and was intimately involved in India's civilian space programme and military missile development efforts.He thus came to be known as the Missile Man of India for his work on the development of ballistic missile and launch vehicle technology.He also played a pivotal organisational, technical, and political role in India's Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998, the first since the original nuclear test by India in 1974.

Biographies

  • Key architect of India's missile development programs, earning the title "Missile Man of India."
  • Played a crucial role in the development and launch of India's first Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III) in 1980, which successfully deployed the Rohini satellite.
  • A pivotal figure in the successful execution of the Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998, making India a nuclear-armed state.
  • Known for his simplicity, accessibility, and dedication to public service, often referred to as the "People's President."
  • Initiated several programs to inspire and mentor young students, including the PURA (Providing Urban Amenities in Rural Areas) initiative.
  • Authored "India 2020: A Vision for the New Millennium," outlining a roadmap for transforming India into a developed nation by 2020.
  • Authored numerous books, including "Wings of Fire," his autobiography, which has inspired millions.
  • "Ignited Minds" and "The Luminous Sparks" emphasize the importance of youth and education in national development.
